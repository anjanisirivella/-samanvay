About Samanvay

Samanvay is an annual three day event brought to you by cultural aficionados in the fields of dance, theatre and music. The event aims to revitalize the essence of India’s heritage in classical forms that appease your soul.

Trance

Day 1 - November 15, 2019

Trance is a quest to present Indian performing traditions in a collage of rhythm, a kaleidoscope of colors, Sensual poetry and traditional Bharatnatyam. The ever-evolving mythology of Siva, the avatars of Vishnu, Gospel of Mary Magdalene among other streams of consciousness is re-contextualized in a form rich in movement and visual imagery. Complimented by a sonic fabric ranging from Indian classical, qawwali, folk and electronic music for audiences to be regaled and transported into the primordial state of being in Trance.

Carnatic 2.0

Day 1 - November 15, 2019

Carnatic 2.0 is an experimental project founded by Mahesh Raghvan that attempts to present Carnatic compositions in a modern style, that would appeal to the young and modern audiences of today.

In this piece, Mahesh, Arjun MPN (flautist), and Akshay Anantapadmanabhan (percussion and konokkol) create a brilliant fusion of different ragas of Carnatic music with various western elements, portraying creativity at its very best.

Maidanam

Day 2 - November 16, 2019

This is the envisioning of the Maidanam, one of the most famous and most controversial of Chalam’s novels, into a stage performance. It dramatises his ideas on marriage, freedom and the moral issues involved in them. He gave a violent jolt to the complacence of the Telugu literary world, and by extension to the Telugu society itself, by writing one controversial novel after another. Sexual freedom beyond the confinements of caste, class and religion especially in the context of women, nullifying marriage as an institution, the duality between love and lust, all are at the core of his volatile writings. He forces you to debate, counter, reflect, recollect, critique, and often juxtaposes you against your own hidden thoughts forcing them to come to surface.

Nibha

Abhinaya (Theatre)

Nibha

Abhinaya (Theatre)

Nibha Theatre Ensemble has come into being in the memory of Ms. Nibha Joshi, a remarkable scholar of arts and aesthetics, who kept reinforcing in her humble ways that every art has a purpose and a true artist is the one who recognizes it and relentlessly tries to accomplish it. Taking inspiration from her philosophical thoughts on art and an artist’s responsibility, the group is an initiative to connect with the theatre enthusiasts of Hyderabad. Theatre essentially is an ensemble art and helps in consolidating the foundations of a pluralistic society like ours.

Birjees

Day 2 - November 16, 2019

Birjees, a tyrannical matriarch rules over her house and five daughters, cruelly crushing their desires and needs. The play eloquently deals with culture of deprivation and denial that manifests itself in violence and hostility which is synonymous with the repression suffered by women in rural or small town women in Indian context till this date. The tragedy of virginity is persistent across the cultural boundaries where sexual and social freedom continues to be a never ending struggle.

A TRIBUTE TO BEGUMS & BAIJIS OF BOLLYWOOD: The First Women of Hindi Cinema

Day 3 - November 17, 2019

A theatrical dance performance by Manjari Chaturvedi that pays homage to early depictions of courtesans in film. This performance is conceptualized, created, and directed by Manjari, with the aim to celebrate the women performers of Bollywood and brings back to life the magic, grace and soul of the Tawaif. This enchanting performance is an ode to erstwhile and now forgotten women performers whose stories will be retold as dramatic interludes throughout the performance.

The panel proudly hosts the following esteemed and celebrated artists and scholars:

Manjari Chaturvedi
Creator of Begum & Baijis of Bollywood, Sufi Kathak dancer, dance choreographer

She has performed in more than 300 concerts in more than 22 countries across the world, from the Taj Mahal to the Sydney Opera House. She has been the part of the Sufi Symposium at the prestigious Smithsonian Museum Washington D.C and performed for short films such as Rumi in the Land of Khusrau, a film on the Sufi traditions across the world.
After the discussion, she will perform her theatrical dance titled Begum & Baijis of Bollywood, where she brings to life the stories of women who were stigmatised by the society for being performers. Manjari says, "It is the need of the hour to question and challenge the 'disregard' towards these women artists and their traditions. We must think about this as a collective society and a project like this helps to shape up the collective conscience of the society."

Shyam Benegal
Director and screenwriter

He was awarded the Padma Shri in 1976, the Padma Bhushan in 1991, and the highest award in Indian cinema for lifetime achievement, the 'Dadasaheb Phalke Award', in 2005. He has won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi seven times and was awarded the 'V. Shantaram Lifetime Achievement Award' at the 2018 Mumbai International Film Festival. He is known for Trikal (Past, Present, Future) (1985), Ankur: The Seedling (1974), and Junoon (1979).

Fauzia Farooqui
Literary scholar and author

She is the President of the South Asian Language Teachers Association (SALTA), an organization that promotes and supports the instruction of South Asian languages in North America. Her published works include Hindustaani Shairaat, an anthology featuring poems by female Urdu poets of India, a monograph on the Urdu prose poem, and two textbooks, Beginning Urdu and Beginning Hindi published by Georgetown University Press.

Yatindra Mishra
Poet, editor, and music & cinema scholar

He is the recipient of the National Film Award 'Swarna-Kamal' for cinema writing, as well as Bhartiya Bhasha Parishad Yuva Puraskar, and the Raza Award. He has published four collections of Hindi poetry, as well as a book on the classical singer Girija Devi called Girija (2001) and a book of conversations with the dancer Sonal Mansingh called Devpriya (2007). In 2012, he published Vismaya Ka Bakhan, a collection of essays on Arts, and a contemporary recreation of vachanas of Akka Mahadevi, a Kannada Lingayat saint poet of the 12th century, in Hindi titled Bhairavi (2012).
